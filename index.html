About me

I am currently an entry-level software engineer at Akamai Technologies, Inc. I graduated from Lafayette College in May 2022. My area of interest revolves around software development, cloud computing, and distributed systems. With a background in electrical and computer engineering, I possess a foundational understanding of computer systems from both hardware and software perspectives. This knowledge proves valuable when designing complex, distributed, and reliable software systems.

Originally from Madagascar, I gained my first internship experience in France. While completing my undergraduate studies in the United States, I also had the opportunity to spend a semester in Germany. Living in diverse locations has taught me the importance of adaptability and flexibility, skills that are particularly crucial in the ever-evolving world of technology.

One achievement I'm proud of from my college experience is co-authoring a research paper titled "Minimally Invasive Monitoring of Cardiac Function for Patients with Rotary VAD Support using the Fourier Transform Approach." Despite lacking a background in biology, I successfully made connections between the functioning of the heart and electrical circuits.

In addition to my technical pursuits, I have a keen interest in mentoring. I aim to inspire younger students, particularly those from underrepresented backgrounds, to develop an interest in and curiosity for STEM fields. Currently, I volunteer as a technical coach for CodePath.org.

Feel free to reach out if you'd like to connect. I am always happy to get a network
